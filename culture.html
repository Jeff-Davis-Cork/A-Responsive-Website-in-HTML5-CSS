The Culture

To dig into the differences a bit more, I have broken the topic down to some of the more common differences and similarities between the US and Ireland.

This is of course assuming that you can categorize the pop, fluffy crap currently in the cinemas and on the airwaves in America as 'culture'.

The Same: Am I really Abroad ??

Another USA?

How other cultures, including Ireland, have adopted the American approach to capitalism is growing. I would use Halloween and Valentine's Day as examples.

For these holidays, and the adoption of commercialization in other parts of society leaves me both feeling at home and alarmed. I won't mention the stupid differences like spelling, drive time and pronunciation.

I'll leave this to shitty conversations that study abroad students have the whole time they are abroad.

The Different: Irish Weird Stuff

Some of the differences in culture are funny and uniquely Irish

I like that shops are not open all night here.

If you insult an Irish person, they'll give it to you back, and not just take it.

As tipping is typically 10 % at restaurants if at all, customer service is optional.

Directions are given using landmarks like trees and everyone is generally up for the craic at any given moment.
